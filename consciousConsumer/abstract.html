Conscious Cosumerism in Sewanee

Calid Shorter and Jennifer Echavarria

Abstract

The production process of consumer goods should be considered when analyzing relationships between businesses. In this regard, our area of interest focuses on the impact of consumerism in McClurg Dining Hall. In order to evaluate the impact of the relationship between McClurg and its suppliers, we will consider factors including: the traveling distance of goods, the commercial size of the business, and the ethical labor involved in its production, which all play essential roles in conscious consumerism. Given this analysis, stakeholders have more information to maximize the University’s positive influence. Furthermore, our project will provide greater transparency and a visual representation for Sewanee’s effort to foster an even greater community by being considerate of the needs, goals, and the resulting relationships conidering these factors, especially those in our extended Appalachian communities.
